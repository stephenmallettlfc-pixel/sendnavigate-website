Where You Are Right Now

You shouldn't have to become a legal expert to get the right help for your child.

But that's exactly what the system asks of you. The Children and Families Act 2014 gave families real legal rights — and put those rights inside a process that can take years to navigate, with two-year clinical waiting lists, a different language at every meeting, and decisions that feel made before you sit down.

If you're here, you've probably already been told no. Or yes — but with a plan that doesn't actually describe your child, doesn't specify the support they need, and doesn't bind anyone to anything.

You don't need a hundred-page handbook. You need someone who knows the system, will look at your specific case, and will tell you the truth about where you stand and what to do next.

I work with parents who are facing:

  • A refusal to carry out a statutory EHCP assessment
  • An EHCP that's vague, weak, or doesn't reflect their child
  • The LA refusing to amend a plan at annual review
  • A school named that you know isn't right
  • An imminent First-tier Tribunal (SEND) hearing
  • Months of unanswered emails and missed deadlines
  • The feeling that nobody is on their side

FAQ

The questions parents ask most

Are you a solicitor?

No. I am a SEND specialist with 25 years of inside experience, not a regulated legal professional. I do not provide legal advice in the regulated sense. What I provide is practical case-building, drafting and coaching grounded in CFA 2014 and SEND tribunal procedure. For many cases that's the level of help families actually need — and at a fraction of the cost of a specialist solicitor. Where a case clearly needs a regulated solicitor, I will tell you.

How long does this take?

It depends on the package. An Initial Consultation is delivered within 7–10 days of you sending documents. Application and Review packages typically run alongside the LA's statutory timeframes, so usually 8–16 weeks. Appeal and Tribunal packages run to the dates set by the tribunal — usually 12–20 weeks from the LA decision letter.

Can you guarantee a win?

No, and anyone who tells you otherwise is selling you something. What I will do is give you an honest assessment of the strengths and risks of your case, build the strongest possible written submission, and prepare you fully for the process. Most parents who lose tribunals lose them on evidence problems that were avoidable — and that's where I work hardest.

What if my child's case is unusual?

I work with the full range of SEND — autism, ADHD, dyslexia, SEMH, speech and language, dyspraxia, moderate learning difficulties, and combinations of all of these. The packages are designed for the structure of the legal process, not for any one diagnosis. Book a free 15-minute call and we'll talk through your specific situation.

Do you cover the whole of England?

Yes. All packages are delivered remotely — video call, email and phone. The First-tier Tribunal (SEND) operates the same statutory framework across England, and most hearings now happen remotely or with a hybrid option. In-person attendance at a hearing is available by separate arrangement.

Is there a payment plan?

For the larger packages (Appeal and Tribunal), yes — typically split across two or three instalments tied to project milestones. We'll discuss what works for your situation on the free call.

What information do you need to start?

For the free 15-minute call, just a brief description of where you are. If we move forward I'll send a short intake form and a secure link for uploading documents — typically the EHCP if you have one, any LA decision letters, recent reports and assessments, and a timeline of what's happened so far.
