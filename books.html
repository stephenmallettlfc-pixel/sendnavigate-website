School Distress & EBSA

Not Refusing. Unable.

"You are not failing." — The UK parent's manual for school distress, EBSA, and the way through

If your child is struggling to attend school, you are not alone — and you are not failing. This book cuts through the confusion around Emotionally Based School Avoidance (EBSA), explains your child's rights under the SEND Code of Practice, and gives you a clear, practical roadmap for getting the right support in place.

Written by a former Headteacher and SENCo who has also navigated this journey as a parent, Not Refusing. Unable. is the honest, jargon-free guide that UK schools and local authorities won't always give you.

What's inside

  • Understanding EBSA vs school refusal
  • Your child's legal rights
  • How to talk to school effectively
  • EHCP applications for EBSA
  • Reintegration strategies that work
  • What to do when school says no
  • Mental health & anxiety support
  • Real parent case examples

About Stephen Mallett

Stephen Mallett spent 25 years working in special educational needs — first as a classroom teacher, then as a SENCo, and eventually as a Headteacher. He has led schools through Ofsted inspections, managed complex EHCP caseloads, and trained hundreds of teachers in SEN best practice.

He is also the father of two children with SEN. That dual perspective — professional expertise combined with lived parental experience — is what makes his books uniquely valuable. He has sat in the meetings you are sitting in. He has fought the battles you are fighting.

Through SEND Navigate, Stephen now offers SEN consultancy directly to families, as well as CPD-accredited training for schools. His books are the starting point — the knowledge base every parent needs before they walk into their next EHCP review or tribunal hearing.

Need More Than a Book?

If you're facing a tribunal, an EHCP refusal, or a school that won't listen, Stephen offers one-to-one consultancy to help you fight back — and win.
