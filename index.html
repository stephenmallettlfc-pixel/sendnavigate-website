Common Questions

What is a McKenzie Friend?
A McKenzie Friend is a lay person who can accompany you to tribunal, take notes, quietly assist you, and — with the Tribunal's permission — speak on your behalf. They are not solicitors but can provide significant practical and moral support throughout the hearing.
Do I need a solicitor?
Not necessarily. Many families achieve the right outcome through well-prepared lay advocacy. We'll be honest with you about whether your case is one where you'd benefit from a solicitor alongside us — and we can signpost you to appropriate resources if so.
What are the Tribunal appeal deadlines?
You must lodge a SEND Tribunal appeal within 2 months of the date of the LA's decision letter, or within 1 month of the date of your mediation certificate — whichever is later. These deadlines are absolute. If you've received a letter, check the date today.
My EHCP has already been issued. Can I still challenge it?
Yes. You can appeal sections B (needs), F (provision), and I (placement) of a final EHCP to the Tribunal. You can also request an early annual review through the school to seek amendments outside the appeal process.
What age does this cover?
EHCPs apply to children and young people from birth to age 25. We work with families across all age ranges and all types of need — autism, ADHD, SEMH, speech and language, complex and multiple needs, and more.
How quickly can you start?
We aim to respond to all enquiries within 24 hours. If you have an urgent deadline, please tell us in your booking form and we'll prioritise your case. Deadlines in SEND are often fixed — we know how to move fast when it matters.
